Methane is the main ingredient in natural gas, and fossil fuel production is a major source of global methane emissions along with agriculture and waste management.For many years, methane was overlooked in the climate change conversation. But scientists and policymakers are increasingly recognizing that methane reductions are crucial. Slowing today’s unprecedented rate of warming can help avert our most acute climate risks, including crop loss, wildfires, extreme weather and rising sea levels.

Methane is a potent greenhouse gas that has more than 80th times the warming power of carbon dioxide over the first 20th years after it reaches the atmosphere. Even though CO2 has a longer-lasting effect, methane sets the pace for warming in the near term.

As the world’s largest oil and gas producer, the United States has both an opportunity and a responsibility to lead the way on reducing methane emissions.
